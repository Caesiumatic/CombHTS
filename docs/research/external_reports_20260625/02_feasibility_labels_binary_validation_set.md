# Electropolymerization Feasibility Labels (YES/NO) in Baseline Nonaqueous Media — A Validation Set for CombHTS

## TL;DR
- I assembled **34 monomer–solvent–electrolyte feasibility rows** from primary electrochemistry literature: **18 NO and 16 YES**, with **22 rows in baseline nonaqueous media** (MeCN/DCM/PC + tetraalkylammonium or LiClO₄ salts), plus **9 matched YES/NO pairs** — exactly the discriminating rows the screen needs to measure its >85% qualitative target.
- The most reliable NO mechanisms in baseline media are: (i) **Eox above the practical solvent/electrolyte anodic window** (thiophene, furan, selenophene, anthracene-flanked monomers); (ii) **radical cations that dimerize to stable, soluble products that diffuse away** rather than coupling to a film (N-/3,6-blocked carbazoles, diphenylamine, aminopyrene, α,α′-disubstituted pyrroles); and (iii) **steric blocking of α–α′ coupling** (3,4-disubstituted thiophenes, 2,5-dimethylpyrrole).
- Feasibility is reported ambiguously across the literature (transient deposit vs. durable electroactive film vs. mere oligomer formation); I scored YES only where a persistent, adherent electroactive film on the electrode is demonstrated, and flagged low/med confidence where the primary source is equivocal.

## Key Findings
1. **Negatives cluster into three mechanistic families.** Unsubstituted thiophene, furan, and selenophene all have monomer oxidation potentials near or above the practical anodic limit of MeCN/TBA-salt media (thiophene "requires potentials above 1.6 V versus SCE… [and] above 1.45–1.55 V would irreversibly damage the conjugated system, due to overoxidation"; selenophene onset 1.83 V vs SCE in MeCN/0.1 M Bu₄NBF₄), so the medium and/or nascent polymer overoxidizes before a quality film forms. These are condition-specific NOs routinely "rescued" in BFEE/Lewis-acid media and are the richest source of matched YES/NO pairs.
2. **Blocking the coupling position converts a YES monomer into an (often intrinsic) NO.** Carbazole electropolymerizes via 3,6-coupling; when both 3 and 6 carry bulky/aryl groups (or oxidation is held below 1.4 V), dimerization is slow (k_dim ≤ 1.5×10⁶ M⁻¹s⁻¹) and "no efficient electropolymerization occurs," whereas 3-ethylcarbazole (k_dim = 1.5×10⁷) "leads to a thick adherent conducting polymer film." α,α′-Disubstituted pyrroles and 3,4-disubstituted thiophenes fail analogously by steric blocking of α–α′ coupling.
3. **Radical-cation stability is the hidden discriminator and the screen's likeliest false-positive source.** Monomers whose radical cations are too stable (diphenylamine, aminopyrene, N-/3,6-blocked carbazoles) couple slowly and the soluble dimer/oligomer diffuses into solution instead of depositing — a NO even though the monomer clearly oxidizes within the window. A pure "Eox-in-window" rule will misclassify all of these as YES.
4. **Aniline is a clean condition-specific pair.** Aniline gives polyaniline films in aqueous/MeCN acid media but yields a poor/insufficient film in neutral MeCN/TBA-salt without added acid ("improvements… obtained by adding anhydrous acid to the medium").
5. **High-Eox / fused-ring and donor–acceptor monomers fail when not "EDOT-capped."** 3-Thiophenecarboxaldehyde "proved to be impossible to electropolymerize" and required an EDOT-flanked trimer; anthracene-flanked EDOT (An-EDOT-An) is "difficult to electropolymerize… in conventional solvents such as acetonitrile (ACN), propylene carbonate (PC) and aqueous system" because of high anthracene oxidation potential plus low solubility, succeeding only as a copolymer or 2.5 V solid device.

## Counts and Pairs
- **Total rows: 34.** YES = 16, NO = 18.
- **Rows in baseline nonaqueous media (MeCN/DCM/PC + TBA salts or LiClO₄): 22.** The remainder are BFEE / aqueous-acid / solid-device "rescue" rows recorded to complete matched pairs and document the special-medium successes (per the project convention: standard-medium failure recorded as NO, special-medium success noted separately).
- **Matched YES/NO pairs (9):**
  1. **Thiophene** — NO in MeCN/TBAClO₄ → YES in BFEE.
  2. **Furan** — NO in MeCN → YES in BFEE/EE.
  3. **Selenophene** — NO in MeCN/Bu₄NBF₄ → YES in BFEE.
  4. **Thiophene vs. bithiophene/terthiophene** (identical PC/1.0 M LiClO₄/Au) — thiophene NO-quality (non-conductive), bi-/terthiophene YES.
  5. **Fluorene** — YES in MeCN (~100% yield) → NO in DCM (~15% yield).
  6. **3-Thiophenecarboxaldehyde** (NO) → **EDOT-flanked trimer** (YES), same DCM/TBAPF₆ conditions.
  7. **Carbazole / 3-ethylcarbazole** (YES) → **3,6-di-tert-butyl- / 3,6-diphenylcarbazole** (NO), identical MeCN conditions.
  8. **Aniline** — NO in neutral MeCN/TBAP → YES in MeCN+acid / aqueous acid.
  9. **Pyrene/aminopyrene** — aminopyrene NO (radical cation diffuses away) → pyrene YES in BFEE.

## Recommendations
1. **Validate against the 9 matched pairs first.** Each isolates a single changing variable (solvent window, coupling position, added acid, conjugation length), so they most directly test whether the screen's three axes (Eox-in-window, anion stability, solubility) predict the flip. If the screen mislabels even **2 of these 9**, the >85% qualitative target is at risk and the ranking weights need retuning before scaling.
2. **Calibrate the solvent-window axis on the thiophene/furan/selenophene NO rows.** These are the cleanest "Eox exceeds practical anodic limit" failures. Set the MeCN/TBA-salt practical anodic cutoff near **~1.6–1.8 V (SCE/Ag-Ag⁺ regime)** and confirm the screen flags monomers above it as NO; the selenophene 1.83 V (MeCN) → 1.23 V (BFEE) shift is a good single-monomer stress test of the medium-dependence logic.
3. **Add a radical-cation-stability / steric-blocking flag — do not leave it optional.** A pure Eox-in-window rule will misclassify N-/3,6-blocked carbazoles, diphenylamine, aminopyrene, and α,α′-disubstituted pyrroles as YES (they oxidize fine within the window) when they are NO. These five rows are the screen's most likely **false positives**; treat them as a dedicated stress test and require the screen to encode a k_dim / coupling-site-availability criterion.
4. **Resolve full-text conditions for the three med-confidence condition-specific rows** (An-EDOT-An exact baseline electrolyte/electrode; diphenylamine concentration threshold; N,N′-diphenylbenzidine) before final scoring, since their NO is sensitive to the precise baseline-medium parameters that determine whether they count as in-scope baseline NOs.
5. **Decision thresholds.** If validated qualitative accuracy is **≥85% on the full 34 and ≥7/9 on the matched pairs**, proceed to expand the screen. If matched-pair accuracy is **<7/9**, hold expansion and make the radical-cation-stability axis mandatory (step 3) before re-testing. If the screen fails any of the three pure solvent-window pairs (1–3), recalibrate the anodic-limit cutoff (step 2) before anything else.

## Caveats
- **"Feasibility" is reported ambiguously.** Sources variously mean (a) a visible deposit, (b) a durable, adherent, redox-stable electroactive film, or (c) merely electroactivity/oligomer formation. I scored **YES only where a persistent, adherent electroactive film on the electrode is demonstrated**; transient deposits, soluble oligomers, and "teardrop"/haze deposits were scored NO. Where a source called something a "film" but described it as low-conductivity/non-electroactive (e.g. polythiophene from thiophene in PC/LiClO₄), I scored the discriminating physical outcome and flagged it.
- **Reference-electrode scales differ** (SCE, Ag/AgCl, Ag/Ag⁺, Fc/Fc⁺); potentials quoted in evidence_summary are **as-reported and not harmonized** — numeric calibration belongs in the separate oxidation-potential artifact, not this binary table.
- **Patent and one conference/patent source** (US5128013, US10197881) are included for distinctive NO observations not found in journals; they are marked med/low confidence because conditions are sometimes incompletely specified. The strongest, highest-confidence NOs are the peer-reviewed carbazole (10.1016/j.elecom.2021.107053), selenophene (10.1016/j.matlet.2004.11.014), thiophene (10.1016/S1388-2481(99)00109-5), furan (10.1016/S0379-6779(98)01509-4), and 3-thiophenecarboxaldehyde (10.1021/acsami.9b21058) rows.
- **Several NO rows are condition-specific, not intrinsic.** The same monomer is often a YES in BFEE/Lewis-acid or aqueous-acid media; those rescues are recorded explicitly so the screen is not penalized for a monomer that is genuinely infeasible only in the baseline medium. The intrinsic NOs (3,4-disubstituted thiophenes, 3,6-blocked carbazoles, α,α′-disubstituted pyrroles, 3-thiophenecarboxaldehyde, aminopyrene) are the ones a correct screen should label NO **regardless of medium**.
- **The An-EDOT-An row's exact baseline electrolyte/electrode for the failed attempt is not in the accessible abstract**; the failure across ACN/PC/aqueous is verbatim from the primary paper (Tao et al., *Chem. Eng. J.* 2016, 10.1016/j.cej.2016.02.048) and the mechanistic reason (high anthracene Eox + low solubility) is corroborated by a sister paper in the same lineage, so it is held at med confidence.
- **SMILES are taken from standard structures where unambiguous and hand-constructed (and valence-checked) for the trimer and An-EDOT-An (formula C₃₄H₂₂O₂S, MW ≈ 494.6); these two and the trimer should be cross-checked before automated ingestion.**
