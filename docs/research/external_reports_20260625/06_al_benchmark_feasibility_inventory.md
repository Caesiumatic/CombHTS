# Feasibility & Data-Inventory Study: A Retrospective Active-Learning Benchmark for Electropolymerization / OMIEC Materials

## TL;DR
- **Feasible, but with caveats.** At least two independent *electropolymerization* experimental datasets exist that can be replayed as retrospective-AL oracles (PANDA PEDOT:PSS electrochromics; the cortisol e-MIP 72-parameter set), and the ~12-million-entry Open Macromolecular Genome (OMG) provides a large, genuinely-open low-fidelity/pretraining source — so the "≥2 independent datasets" bar to avoid single-dataset overclaiming is met.
- **The experimental oracles are small** (PANDA = 48 systematic LHS + 20 AL points; cortisol e-MIP = 72 parameter sets with replicates). This suits a "how few experiments do you need?" framing but limits any claim of broad generalizability; the Polybot ECP color database adds a third, larger but compositionally-defined target.
- **The central gap claim holds: as of mid-2026 there is no canonical, shared, publicly released OMIEC / electropolymerization active-learning benchmark.** Existing AL/BO harnesses (Olympus, Olympus-enhanced, Atlas) contain no electropolymerization surface, and 2025–2026 benchmarking efforts do not contest this.

## Details

### 1. PANDA — PEDOT:PSS electrochromics (primary oracle candidate)
Confirmed directly from the Materials Horizons full text (Open Access, CC-BY) and arXiv 2406.17725. The experimental design matches the task description verbatim: the team "employed Latin hypercube sampling (LHS) to select 16 distinct experiments at each of three EDOT concentrations (0.01, 0.03, and 0.1 M), totaling 48 experiments. The parameters ranged from 0.8 to 1.6 V for the deposition potential and from 1 to 100 seconds for the deposition time, with the latter being selected in log space" (Robben et al., Mater. Horiz. 2024, 11, 5331–5340). The target observable is confirmed as ΔE00 — "a color difference metric by CIE … reflecting the human-perceived color change" (CIEDE2000), with the total reduction charge Q as a secondary measured quantity. After the 48-point LHS training set, the team ran a Bayesian-optimization AL campaign; the paper states "After 20 rounds of experimentation, the updated model predictions indicated a refined understanding of the parameter space," using a GPR surrogate with expected-improvement acquisition. The optimum found was ΔE00 ≈ 21.7 ± 0.7 (predicted) vs. 22.2 ± 1.2 (observed).

**Accessibility caveat:** The paper is fully open and code is in the ESI / github.com/BU-KABlab/PANDA-BEAR, but I could not confirm a standalone deposited tabular dataset (e.g., a Zenodo CSV) of the 48+20 ΔE00 points; the data appears embedded in the manuscript/ESI and code repository. For benchmark construction the data is recoverable but may need extraction rather than a one-click download — flag this as "open but not packaged."

This is the single best fit for a retrospective-AL oracle: a small, systematically-sampled, continuous + categorical design space with a clean scalar objective, explicitly built as an SDL/AL campaign.

### 2. PANDA-film — wetting (weak oracle)
Confirmed from arXiv 2601.07043 (Quinn et al., to appear Rev. Sci. Instrum. 2026). The target observable is **water contact angle** (a proxy for surface energy), measured by a novel top-down reflectance method; the model polymer is poly(allyl methacrylate) (PAMA) deposited by electrodeposition of polymer networks (EPoN). The Data Availability statement reads: "All data is present in the manuscript. All software and 3D models are available at github.com/BU-KABlab/PANDA-BEAR." This is a hardware/methods validation paper — it does not release a systematic, densely-sampled design-space dataset, so it is not usable as a standalone AL oracle, though it confirms a second observable (wetting) the PANDA platform can generate.

### 3. OMG + OMG_PhysicalProperties — low-fidelity / pretraining source
The OMG (Kim, Schroeder, Jackson, ACS Polym. Au 2023) is a synthetically-aware database. Its scale is precisely stated: "Based on the selected 77,281 reactants in the OMG, nearly 12 million chemically distinct CRUs corresponding to these reactants were generated by applying the 17 template-based polymerization algorithms implemented with RDKit." The proof-of-concept used LogP and ~150,000 sampled polymers for generative modeling. The companion "Functional monomer design" paper (Chem. Sci. 2025, 16, 4755–4767, DOI 10.1039/D4SC08617A) "explore[s] functional monomer design by developing the first comprehensive database of monomer-level chemical and physical properties for approximately 12M synthetically accessible polymers." Confirmed details:
- **Properties:** "A comprehensive set of 25 monomer-level properties for OMG polymers was prepared, encompassing chemistry descriptors, molecular flexibility, geometry descriptors, electronic properties, optical properties, and phase behavior descriptors" (Table 1 gives symbol, description, unit, category). All geometric/electronic/optical properties are from DFT single-point calculations Boltzmann-averaged over up to five conformers of methyl-terminated CRUs. Confirmed specific properties include radius of gyration (Rg), energy of the lowest singlet excited state (ES1), and Flory–Huggins χ parameters (e.g., χwater) estimated from COSMO-SAC σ-profiles. (Note: a results figure references "all 19 monomer-level properties," vs. 25 in Table 1 — resolve against Table 1 directly before quoting a specific count.)
- **Uncertainty fields: YES.** Properties were generated via uncertainty-guided active learning: "Approximately 12 000 OMG CRUs (≈0.1% of the OMG chemical space) were randomly sampled for each polymerization mechanism (i.e., stratified random sampling) as an initial dataset," then DFT-labeled to train directed message-passing neural networks with evidential learning (D-MPNN evidential networks), which "estimated prediction uncertainties for monomer-level properties" — so per-property uncertainty is included.
- **Download:** Zenodo record 10.5281/zenodo.13863778 (v2, published Dec 1 2024), total 52.1 GB, with files such as OthersWithREADME.zip (6.0 GB) and pareto_greedy_*.tar.gz; 32,529 QM-calculated geometries (pareto-greedy) + 15,147 test (per the Zenodo README's de-duplication notes). Fully open, direct download. Code: github.com/TheJacksonLab/OpenMacromolecularGenome and OMG_PhysicalProperties.

Relevance to electropolymerization: the electronic properties (HOMO/LUMO-type descriptors and oxidation/ionization-related quantities), optical properties, and COSMO-SAC solubility/χ parameters are plausibly correlated with monomer oxidation/polymerization propensity and film solubility — making OMG a credible **low-fidelity featurization / pretraining source** rather than an experimental oracle.

### 4. Polybot ECP database
Confirmed (Wu, Vriza et al., JACS 2025, 147, 44101–44113, DOI 10.1021/jacs.5c12241; ChemRxiv 10.26434/chemrxiv-2025-9pqc0). The work used LLM-assisted literature mining to build "a publicly accessible ECP informatics database" plus a physics-informed copolymer ML model, and Polybot autonomously hit color targets "fine-tuning copolymer structures with a 5% step size in comonomer composition within a three-monomer system." The data/code are in public, MIT-licensed GitHub repos (polybot-nexus/EPoly_LitMining_ColorPred and co-polymer-set-transformer) plus a Streamlit app. **Schema (confirmed verbatim from the co-polymer-set-transformer README):** columns `smiles1, percentage_1, smiles2, percentage_2, smiles3, percentage_3, L, a, b, wavelength, intensity` — i.e., up to three monomer SMILES with composition ratios as inputs, and CIELab color + full absorption spectrum (wavelength/intensity lists) as targets; the last two columns are optional (CIELab-only mode). It is multi-objective (color + spectrum). **Exact size is NOT confirmable from primary sources** — the CSV/notebook contents (`database_analysis.ipynb`, `electrochromics_in_house_experiments_with_abs.csv`) were inaccessible to automated tools, and the "more than a thousand possible compositions" figure describes the autonomous search space, not the database row count. Note also this is solution copolymer synthesis (e.g., direct arylation), not strictly electrodeposition.

### 5. Olympus / Olympus-enhanced / Atlas (benchmarking harnesses)
Olympus (Häse et al., MLST 2021) provides 10 core experimental-emulator datasets, confirmed from the official docs: alkox (reaction rate, max), colors_bob (green-ness, min), colors_n9 (green-ness, min), fullerenes (yield, max), hplc (peak area, max), benzylation (e_factor, min), photo_pce10 (stability, min), photo_wf3 (stability, min), snar (e_factor, min), suzuki (yield, max). Community datasets are downloadable via `olympus download -n <name>` (e.g., excitonics). **None are electropolymerization or OMIEC.** The closest polymer-relevant surfaces are **photo_pce10 / photo_wf3** (photostability of organic-photovoltaic/conjugated-polymer materials — PCE10 is a conjugated polymer donor, but the task is photodegradation, not electropolymerization) and **colors_bob / colors_n9** (color optimization of dye/pigment mixtures). Olympus-enhanced (ChemRxiv 10.26434/chemrxiv-2023-74w8d) adds mixed-parameter/multi-objective benchmarks but no electropolymerization surface. **Atlas** (Hickman et al., Digital Discovery 2025, 4, 1006–1029, DOI 10.1039/D4DD00115J) is a BO *planner* ("brain"), not a dataset; it was demonstrated optimizing the oxidation potential of metal complexes on an autonomous electrochemical platform — electrochemistry-adjacent but not polymer/electropolymerization. Both are accessed via open GitHub Python packages and are the natural harness in which to *run* a new benchmark.

### 6. Adjacent electrochemistry datasets
- **Electrolab** (Oh, Pence, Lukhanin, Rodríguez, Schroeder, Rodríguez-López, Device 2023, 1(5), 100103, DOI 10.1016/j.device.2023.100103): open-source modular platform for **redox-active electrolyte** characterization. The abstract states: "We demonstrate the Electrolab by autonomously analyzing a redox mediator by performing 200 voltammograms and data analysis steps across a range of conditions." "Data from 'The Electrolab'" is posted at Mendeley Data / Zenodo. It is a characterization platform for electrolytes/redox mediators, not an electropolymerization synthesis-design dataset, so it is not a polymer AL oracle. (A related platform, "eLab," later collected >10,000 CVs for enzymatic electrochemistry — also not polymer synthesis.)
- **Cortisol e-MIP** (Dykstra, Reynolds, Smith, Zhou, Liu, ACS Appl. Mater. Interfaces 2022, 14, 25972–25983, DOI 10.1021/acsami.2c02474): confirmed verbatim — "e-MIPs are fabricated with 72 sets of synthesis parameters with replicates. Their sensing performances are measured using a 12-channel potentiostat… The Gaussian process (GP) is employed as the mainstay of the integrated framework." The synthesis/condition variables (corroborated by the related Dykstra MTU dissertation and MIP literature) are pyrrole monomer concentration, cortisol template concentration, CV electropolymerization cycles, overoxidation, and CV scan rate. The measured target is the sensor response / imprinting factor (DPV current response). This is a **genuine electropolymerization design-space dataset** suitable as a second oracle; the 72-set data appears in the paper/SI rather than a confirmed standalone repository, so extraction may be needed.

### Critical gap check (mid-2026)
A fresh review of 2025–2026 literature finds **no** canonical, shared, publicly released OMIEC/electropolymerization active-learning benchmark:
- The 2025 ACS Chem. Rev. "Electropolymerization of Organic Mixed Ionic-Electronic Conductors: Fundamentals and Applications in Bioelectronics" (Gerasimov et al., DOI 10.1021/acs.chemrev.5c00183) is a fundamentals/applications review — it surveys the field but releases no AL benchmark dataset.
- "Benchmarking self-driving labs" (Adesiji, Wang, Kuo, Brown, Digital Discovery 2026, 5, 14–27, DOI 10.1039/D5DD00337G; arXiv 2508.06642) proposes acceleration/enhancement-factor metrics across SDLs but includes no electropolymerization surface or shared oracle.
- "The Open Polymers 2026 (OPoly26) Dataset" (arXiv 2512.23117) is a 6.35-million-entry DFT dataset for polymer atomistic ML — not an AL benchmark and not electropolymerization.
- "Agentic AI for Self-Driving Laboratories in Soft Matter" (arXiv 2601.17920) is a taxonomy/benchmark-perspective paper, not a released electropolymerization benchmark.
- Olympus / Olympus-enhanced / Atlas contain no electropolymerization surface (above).

**Verdict: CONFIRMED.** No existing canonical OMIEC/electropolymerization AL benchmark contests the gap claim. The planned paper occupies genuinely open ground.

## Verdict on ≥2-dataset feasibility
**Yes — the requirement is met.** Two *bona fide electropolymerization* experimental datasets qualify as independent oracles:
1. **PANDA PEDOT:PSS electrochromics** (48 LHS + 20 AL points; ΔE00 objective; 3-variable design space; thiophene-based oxidative electrodeposition).
2. **Cortisol e-MIP** (72 parameter sets; sensor-response/imprinting-factor objective; 5-variable design space; pyrrole-based electropolymerization).

These differ in monomer chemistry (EDOT/PEDOT vs. pyrrole/polypyrrole), objective (electrochromic contrast vs. molecular-recognition sensing), and dimensionality (3 vs. 5 factors) — genuine independence that defends against the "single dataset, overclaiming" critique. The **Polybot ECP database** (multi-objective color/spectrum, 3-monomer copolymer composition) provides an optional third target to demonstrate scaling, contingent on confirming its exact size.

## Primary oracle vs. low-fidelity source
- **Primary high-fidelity ORACLE:** **PANDA (PEDOT:PSS, ΔE00).** It is the cleanest replayable AL campaign — small, systematically LHS-sampled over a defined 3-D design space, with a single clean scalar objective, and it was *itself* an SDL/Bayesian-optimization campaign, so replaying held-out points as sequential "queries" is methodologically faithful. The cortisol e-MIP serves as the **second, independent oracle**.
- **LOW-FIDELITY / PRETRAINING source:** **OMG_PhysicalProperties** (≈12M monomers, 25 DFT/COSMO-SAC monomer-level properties with evidential uncertainties). Pretrain or featurize on computed monomer electronic/optical/solubility descriptors that plausibly correlate with oxidation/polymerization propensity, then fine-tune on the small experimental oracles. This multi-fidelity coupling is the most defensible differentiator for the methods paper.

## Recommendations

**Stage 1 — Lock in the two-oracle core (now).**
- Adopt **PANDA** as the primary oracle. First action: reconstruct the 48+20 ΔE00 table from the ESI/figures and the BU-KABlab/PANDA-BEAR repo, and open a GitHub issue / email the authors (Brown/Werner labs) to obtain the raw CSV and remove the "open-but-not-packaged" risk.
- Adopt the **cortisol e-MIP 72-parameter set** as the second oracle. First action: extract the 72-set table + replicates from the ACS AMI 2022 SI; confirm the exact target definition (imprinting factor vs. raw DPV ΔI).

**Stage 2 — Add breadth and the pretraining axis.**
- Add the **Polybot ECP database** as a third, larger, multi-objective (color + spectrum) target — *conditional on confirming its exact row count* (clone polybot-nexus repos, run `pandas .shape`, read `database_analysis.ipynb`).
- Wire in **OMG_PhysicalProperties** as the low-fidelity pretraining/featurization source.

**Stage 3 — Implement in a standard harness.**
- Wrap each oracle as an Olympus `Dataset`/emulator and run planners through **Atlas** (mixed-parameter, multi-objective, multi-fidelity BO) so results are directly comparable to the established SDL-AL literature.

**Thresholds that would change the plan:**
- If PANDA's raw dataset cannot be recovered cleanly → promote Polybot ECP (after size confirmation) to second core oracle and treat PANDA as illustrative.
- If the cortisol e-MIP SI yields too few usable rows after removing replicates/failures → rely on PANDA + Polybot ECP.
- If the Polybot ECP database proves to contain only a few dozen *experimental* rows (vs. literature-mined) → keep it as a multi-objective stress test, not a generalizability claim.

## Caveats
- **Small oracle sizes.** All true electropolymerization oracles are small (PANDA = 68; cortisol e-MIP = 72). This *suits* the "how few experiments?" framing but precludes strong generalizability claims; state this explicitly to pre-empt the overclaiming critique.
- **Packaging vs. openness.** PANDA's tabular ΔE00 data and the cortisol e-MIP 72-set data are open in principle (paper/ESI/SI) but not deposited as clean standalone CSV/Zenodo records; budget time for extraction and author contact. Polybot ECP repos are openly clonable but the exact row count is unconfirmed from primary sources.
- **Domain-mismatch caveats.** Polybot ECP is solution copolymer synthesis (direct arylation), not electrodeposition; OMG is computed monomer properties, not experimental electropolymerization; Electrolab is electrolyte characterization. Each is useful for breadth/pretraining but should not be mislabeled as electropolymerization oracles.
- **Property-count discrepancy in OMG.** The Chem. Sci. 2025 text cites 25 monomer-level properties (Table 1) but a results figure references 19; confirm the exact set and units against Table 1 before publishing specific descriptors (e.g., any oxidation/ionization-potential proxy).
- **Olympus polymer surfaces are photostability, not synthesis.** photo_pce10/photo_wf3 involve a conjugated polymer but measure photodegradation, so they cannot substitute for an electropolymerization oracle.
- **Gap-claim scope.** The "no benchmark" verdict is robust as of mid-2026 but is inherently a negative claim; phrase it as "to our knowledge, no publicly released benchmark exists," since new releases could appear.
