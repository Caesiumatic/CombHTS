## Mechanistic framing (why these are clean, not arbitrary)
Electropolymerizability is gated by radical-cation coupling at the reactive (Оұ for 5-membered heterocycles; 3,6 for carbazole; para for arylamine) positions. A monomer fails to form a film when either (i) those positions are **blocked** (Оұ-dimethyl thiophene; para-substituted triarylamine), or (ii) the radical cation is **too stabilized to couple fast enough** (3,6-dialkyl/diaryl/di-tert-butyl carbazoles; trianisylamine). Both failure modes leave a clean CV oxidation wave but no per-cycle current growth вҖ” the exact "oxidizes-but-no-film" pattern the screen must not mis-score as YES. All seven rows are in MeCN (or DCM) with tetraalkylammonium salts on Pt, matching the screen's baseline assumption.

## Per-class notes
**Carbazoles (rows 32вҖ“35).** The Г–nal 2021 study is the single most useful reference in the whole feasibility set because it provides a *quantitative* dividing line (kdim вүҲ 1.5Г—10вҒ· вҶ’ film; вүӨ 1.5Г—10вҒ¶ вҶ’ no film) and a YES/NO ladder under identical conditions: 3-ethylcarbazole (YES) vs 3,6-diethyl / 3-tert-butyl / 3,6-di-tert-butyl / 3-phenyl / 3,6-diphenyl (NO under 1.4 V). The 3-phenyl and 3,6-diphenyl cases carry a potential-window caveat (they DO react above ~1.7 V via phenyl-centered radicals, but that is an uncontrolled degradative regime, not a clean film) вҖ” record them as NO-in-the-controlled-window.
**Triarylamines (rows 36вҖ“37).** These extend research3's triphenylamine case (row 25, dimer-only вҶ’ tetraphenylbenzidine) to the fully-blocked limit: with all three para positions capped (OMe, Br), even the dimerization is shut off, giving a persistent reversible radical cation that is sold/used as an oxidant or redox-flow catholyte. Mechanism-based (Tier B) but extremely well established.
**Оұ-blocked thiophene (row 38).** 2,5-dimethylthiophene is the textbook Оұ-blocked monomer: it cannot do the ОұвҖ“Оұ coupling that builds polythiophene, so electropolymerization in MeCN does not give a conducting film. Tier B because the cleanest statements describe chemical (not electrochemical) ОІвҖ“ОІ routes; the electro-NO is the standard inference.
