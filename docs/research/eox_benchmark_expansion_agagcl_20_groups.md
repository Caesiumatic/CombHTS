# Expanding the Monomer Oxidation-Potential Benchmark: Strict Ag/AgCl Curation Report

**Bottom line:** Six (6) net-new, defensible strict Ag/AgCl monomer-oxidation calibration groups were found and curated, raising the strict collapsed total from 14 to **20** — short of the ≥30 target. Reaching ≥30 from clean, Ag/AgCl-native, non-polymerization, unambiguous-SMILES, auditable-locator primary data is **not achievable without policy relaxation**; the maximum defensible strict set is 20, and the specific relaxations needed to grow it are spelled out below. Do not pad to 30 with polymerization onsets, pseudo-reference values, or speculative reference conversions.

---

## TL;DR
- **6 net-new strict Ag/AgCl groups** are defensible now (3 high-confidence in acetonitrile; 3 medium-confidence in dichloromethane with flagged gaps), taking the strict benchmark from **14 → 20**. The ≥30 target is **NOT met**.
- The binding constraint is **not contamination but scarcity of clean primary data that is simultaneously (a) natively Ag/AgCl, (b) a genuine soluble-monomer oxidation feature, (c) structurally unambiguous (SMILES), and (d) auditable to a specific table/figure.** Most electropolymerization literature fails one of these (Ag/Ag⁺ pseudo-reference, SCE, Fc/Fc⁺, polymerization onsets, or POSS/complex structures).
- **Recommended action: hybrid A+B** — commit the 6 strict rows now, AND ask the PI to approve a *separate* Fc/Fc⁺ master-scale CSV plus targeted full-text retrieval of the Reynolds-school / OPV-OLED HOMO-from-CV literature, which natively reports Fc/Fc⁺ and is the only realistic route to ≥30. Do **not** abandon expansion (option C).

---

## Key Findings
1. **The Cihaner / Önal / Atılım donor–acceptor–donor (D-A-D) family is the single richest clean Ag/AgCl source.** This group consistently reports *first-scan irreversible monomer oxidation peaks* explicitly vs Ag/AgCl in well-defined nonaqueous media before film growth — exactly the calibration feature required. Five of the six promoted rows come from this family.
2. **A task-premise error was found and corrected.** The original task listed FSeF = 1.16 V and FSF = 1.06 V. The full text of Polym. Chem. 2013, 4, 2457 states the opposite: **FSeF = 1.06 V, FSF = 1.16 V** vs Ag/AgCl (the Se acceptor is weaker → more electron-rich monomer → lower oxidation potential, consistent with the paper's own reasoning). The corrected assignment is used here.
3. **OSeO and FSeF are the same molecule measured in different solvents.** OSeO (J. Electroanal. Chem. 2016, 780, 84) and FSeF (Polym. Chem. 2013) are both 4,7-di(furan-2-yl)benzo[c][1,2,5]selenadiazole. Because the grouping key is canonical_SMILES + solvent_name + label_type, the DCM measurement (1.15 V) and the ACN measurement (1.06 V) are **two legitimately distinct collapsed groups** — directly analogous to the existing Camarada ACN/DCM solvent pairs in the accepted 14.
4. **A second task-premise correction:** the OSeO/SSeS/SeSeSe paper is in *Journal of Electroanalytical Chemistry* 2016, 780, 84–89 (DOI 10.1016/j.jelechem.2016.08.039), **not** Organic Electronics as the pii might suggest.
5. **Large quantities of nearby data are real but ineligible or candidate-only:** furan/2-methylfuran (1.75/1.50 V vs **Ag/Ag⁺ pseudo-reference**), ProDOP-BuOH (0.72 V vs **Ag/Ag⁺**), carbazole/N-ethylcarbazole (~1.06–1.20 V vs **SCE**), thieno[3,4-b]furan (peak 1.2 V vs **Ag/Ag⁺**), and numerous EDOT/ProDOT/EDOP polymerization setpoints. None of these can enter the strict Ag/AgCl CSV without a source-internal conversion or relabeling.
6. **The Fc/Fc⁺ track is scientifically preferable for nonaqueous CV** (Fc/Fc⁺ is the IUPAC-recommended internal nonaqueous standard, immune to junction-potential drift that plagues aqueous Ag/AgCl bridged into organic solvent), but within this search no *clean primary monomer-oxidation peak natively reported vs Fc/Fc⁺* was isolated. The unlocking potential is real but lives in literature not fully mined here.
