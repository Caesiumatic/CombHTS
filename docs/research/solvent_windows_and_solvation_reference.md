# Solvent Library Reference Table: Electrochemical Windows & Solvation Properties for an Electropolymerization Screening Pipeline

## TL;DR
- The single most important screening quantity — the **anodic (oxidation) limit** — is **electrode/electrolyte/reference dependent**; for the two priority conventional solvents the best-documented Pt values are **nitrobenzene ≈ +1.6 to +2.0 V vs SCE** and **benzonitrile = +1.8 V vs SCE** (with cathodic limits of ≈ −1.0 to −1.1 V and −1.95 V vs SCE respectively), giving benzonitrile the wider and more balanced usable window.
- **SMD is ready out-of-the-box for 4 of the conventional solvents** (NitroBenzene, BenzoNitrile, NitroMethane, and sulfolane as "TetraHydroThiophene-s,s-dioxide"); **PC, GBL, and NMP are NOT named SMD solvents** and must be entered as custom `SCRF=(SMD,Solvent=Generic,Read)` definitions.
- **All five "exotic" solvents (3 ILs, the Reline DES, and BFEE) should be EXCLUDED from a continuum-only (SMD/COSMO/ALPB) screen or treated with explicit solvation** — they are ionic/structured/Lewis-acid media that continuum models represent poorly; BFEE is special because it actively LOWERS monomer oxidation potentials and is the preferred electropolymerization medium.

## Key Findings

1. **Anodic limit is the gating quantity and it is condition-bound.** Every literature window must carry: reference electrode, supporting-electrolyte salt + concentration, working-electrode material, and (ideally) the current-density cutoff. The practical edge is set by "an arbitrary current density cutoff" and "the WE material has a significant impact on the observed decomposition kinetics" (Ruschhaupt et al., *Batteries & Supercaps* 2020, doi:10.1002/batt.202000009); the field-standard cutoff is commonly 0.1 mA/cm². BDD electrodes inflate windows dramatically — a bare BDD electrode exhibits a ~3.7 V solvent window versus ~1.0 V for glassy carbon in the same study (Macpherson et al.), i.e. ~2.7 V wider — and so a BDD-derived window must NEVER be presented as the generic solvent window. Pt and glassy carbon (GC) are the electropolymerization-relevant materials and are prioritized below.

2. **SMD parametrization is binary for screening purposes.** From the Gaussian 16 built-in solvent list: NitroBenzene (ε=34.809), BenzoNitrile (ε=25.592), NitroMethane (ε=36.562), and sulfolane listed as "TetraHydroThiophene-s,s-dioxide" (ε=43.962) are all directly usable. PC, GBL, and NMP are absent and require the 7-descriptor custom definition (Eps, EpsInf=n², HbondAcidity α, HbondBasicity β, SurfaceTensionAtInterface γ, CarbonAromaticity φ, ElectronegativeHalogenicity ψ). The authoritative parametrization reference is Marenich, Cramer & Truhlar, *J. Phys. Chem. B* 2009, 113, 6378.

3. **Exotic solvents break the continuum assumption.** ILs ([BMIM][PF6], [BMIM][BF4], [EMIM][TFSI]), the choline chloride:urea DES (Reline), and BFEE are themselves the electrolyte (or a Lewis-acid complex). Their wide apparent windows arise from ionic/interfacial decomposition reactions, not molecular HOMO/LUMO gaps, and εr/SMD descriptors are ill-defined. These belong in a separate handling branch.

## Per-Solvent Notes

### How electrode and reference choice shift each window
- **Working electrode:** BDD > GC > Pt for window width in most media. The effect is large and quantified: a bare BDD electrode showed a ~3.7 V solvent window versus ~1.0 V for glassy carbon under the same conditions (Macpherson et al.) — i.e. BDD added ~2.7 V of apparent range purely by suppressing solvent-breakdown kinetics. A BDD-derived window must therefore never be used as the "generic" solvent value for an electropolymerization screen (electropolymerization is almost always done on Pt, GC, or ITO). Pt tends to give the narrowest cathodic limit (facile H/proton reduction); GC extends the cathodic side. The practical edge is always defined by an arbitrary current-density cutoff (field-standard ~0.1 mA/cm²) and the WE material strongly affects the observed decomposition kinetics (Ruschhaupt et al., *Batteries & Supercaps* 2020, doi:10.1002/batt.202000009).
- **Supporting electrolyte:** The salt anion sets the practical anodic limit (ClO4⁻, PF6⁻, BF4⁻ are anodically robust); the cation (typically tetrabutylammonium) sets the cathodic limit. Always 0.1 M unless otherwise stated. Impurity water/oxygen narrows windows substantially.
- **Reference electrode and conversions used:** Convert to Ag/AgCl only when a calibrant exists. Conventions adopted here:
  - **Fc/Fc⁺ → Ag/AgCl ≈ +0.45 V in MeCN** (flagged ±0.1 V; solvent-dependent).
  - **Fc/Fc⁺ → SCE ≈ +0.40 V in MeCN.**
  - **SCE → SHE = +0.241 V; Ag/AgCl (sat. KCl) → SHE = +0.197 V; thus SCE ≈ Ag/AgCl + 0.044 V.**
  - For ILs with Ag or Ag/AgCl **quasi-references**, potentials are internally consistent only within that study; the [EMIM][TFSI] Ag/AgCl QRE was calibrated at −0.355 V vs Fc/Fc⁺ (298.15 K, +0.65 mV/K).

### Exotic-solvent modeling caveats and recommended treatment
- **Ionic liquids ([BMIM][PF6], [BMIM][BF4], [EMIM][TFSI]):** These ARE the electrolyte; there is no separate supporting salt. Continuum models (SMD, COSMO, ALPB) assume a structureless dielectric and fail to capture the strong ion–ion correlation, local electric fields, and specific cation/anion interactions that determine real solvation and the electrochemical window (which is governed by anion oxidation and cation reduction, not a molecular HOMO/LUMO gap). **Recommendation: exclude from a continuum-only screen, or model with explicit IL ions (cluster/QM-MM/MD) if IL media are essential.** A high-ε continuum (ε ≈ 12–15) is sometimes used as a crude placeholder, but it is not predictive for oxidation potentials and should be flagged low-confidence.
- **Reline DES (ChCl:urea 1:2):** A hydrogen-bonded eutectic in which chloride–urea and choline interactions dominate. εr is not cleanly defined (broadband dielectric spectroscopy gives method-dependent ~30–50; no authoritative single 25 °C scalar). Extremely viscous (~1750 cP). **Recommendation: exclude from continuum screen; requires explicit H-bond-network treatment.**
- **BFEE (BF3·OEt2):** Not a passive solvent — it is a middle-strength Lewis acid that **coordinates to the monomer (through S or π systems), reduces its aromaticity/resonance energy, and thereby lowers its oxidation potential**, enabling electropolymerization at mild potentials and producing high-quality, mechanically strong films. This catalytic effect is its entire reason for use. Continuum solvation cannot represent a covalent/dative Lewis-acid–monomer adduct; **the BFEE–monomer complex must be modeled explicitly (the adduct as the actual electroactive species).** Documented effect sizes: a carbazole-fluorene monomer oxidized at +0.47 V in 40% BFEE/CH2Cl2 vs +1.07 V in pure CH2Cl2; naphthalene at ~1.0 V vs SCE in BFEE/TFA vs 1.55 V vs SCE in MeCN.

### SMD usage summary for the pipeline
- **Drop-in named solvents:** `Solvent=NitroBenzene`, `Solvent=BenzoNitrile`, `Solvent=NitroMethane`, `Solvent=TetraHydroThiophene-s,s-dioxide` (sulfolane).
- **Custom `Generic,Read` definitions required:** PC, GBL, NMP. Provide all 7 descriptors (Eps, EpsInf=n², HbondAcidity, HbondBasicity, SurfaceTensionAtInterface, CarbonAromaticity, ElectronegativeHalogenicity). The Abraham α/β values quoted above are approximate and should be verified against the Minnesota Solvent Descriptor Database before production runs.
- **Exclude from continuum screen:** all three ILs, Reline, and BFEE.

## Recommendations
1. **Stage 1 — run the screen only on continuum-valid solvents** with confident SMD parametrization: nitrobenzene, benzonitrile, nitromethane, sulfolane (named); PC, GBL, NMP (custom, after verifying Abraham descriptors). Use each solvent's **Pt/GC anodic limit** as the hard ceiling: a monomer's computed oxidation potential must fall **below** the anodic limit with a **≥0.2–0.3 V safety margin** to absorb the ±0.1 V reference-conversion and the cutoff-criterion uncertainty.
2. **Prioritize benzonitrile and nitrobenzene** for aromatic/radical-cation-forming monomers — wide anodic range and strong radical-cation stabilization. Benzonitrile is the cleanest, most balanced choice (+1.8/−1.95 V vs SCE on Pt). Use **nitromethane or sulfolane** when maximal anodic stability is needed, but note nitromethane's volatility/safety and sulfolane's mp >25 °C (run >30 °C or as cosolvent).
3. **Handle BFEE as a dedicated low-oxidation-potential route**, modeling the BFEE–monomer adduct explicitly; flag any monomer that fails the anodic-ceiling test in conventional solvents as a BFEE candidate (oxidation potentials commonly drop 0.4–0.6 V on Lewis-acid coordination).
4. **Quarantine the ILs and Reline** into an explicit-solvation sub-pipeline or exclude them; do not let a continuum εr placeholder generate oxidation-potential predictions that feed compatibility decisions.
5. **Benchmarks that would change the staging:** (a) if a validated explicit-IL solvation scheme (cluster-continuum or MD-derived) reproduces measured IL windows within ~0.3 V, promote the ILs into the main screen; (b) if verified Abraham descriptors for PC/GBL/NMP differ materially from the placeholders above, re-run those rows; (c) if a future BDD-based dataset is the only window source for a solvent, do not adopt it — fall back to Pt/GC values or flag the solvent as unscreenable.

## Caveats
- **Window edges are not universal constants.** Reported anodic/cathodic limits vary with electrode, electrolyte, water/oxygen content, scan rate, and the (often unstated) current-density cutoff. Treat all single numbers as condition-specific, and prefer the explicitly-conditioned Pt/GC entries.
- **Flagged low-confidence values:** the nitrobenzene anodic edge and the PC/GBL/NMP/nitromethane window edges are approximate ranges where the original cutoff criterion was not standardized. The **benzonitrile entry (IUPAC *Pure Appl. Chem.* 1987, 59, 703) is the single cleanest conventional datapoint** — fully conditioned (Pt, 0.1 M TBAP, vs SCE).
- **Supplier (IoLiTec) IL windows lack a stated reference electrode** and are flagged accordingly; the peer-reviewed [EMIM][TFSI] and [BMIM][BF4] values carry quasi-reference caveats.
- **Reline's "electrochemical window"** in the cited EES paper is a supercapacitor cell voltage (2.2 V) measured vs Hg/Hg2SO4, NOT a GC/Pt half-cell ESW vs Fc/Fc⁺; the "3–4 V" figure is secondary literature. Reline's εr has no authoritative single 25 °C value (method-dependent ~30–50).
- **Abraham hydrogen-bond descriptors** for the custom SMD solvents (PC, GBL, NMP) are approximate; verify against the Minnesota Solvent Descriptor Database (the authoritative source feeding SMD) before relying on absolute ΔG_solv values.
- **Computational windows (e.g., MD+DFT studies such as *Chem. Mater.* 2011, doi:10.1021/cm200679y) are not measurements** and were not used as experimental window values here; that study also concerns BMIM, not EMIM, and underestimates experimental windows.
- **Refractive-index conflict resolved:** the peer-reviewed n_D ≈ 1.42 for [BMIM][BF4] (Soriano 2007) is adopted; the supplier-listed "1.52" appears erroneous and was not used.
