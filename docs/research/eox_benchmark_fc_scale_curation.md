## Tolerances and limitations

I applied **one and only one proposed tolerance**, and I did **not** apply it to the strict CSV. The tolerance is: accept monomer oxidation values that are **reported directly versus Fc/Fc+** when the paper states that the nonaqueous reference electrode was **calibrated or verified with ferrocene**, even though the paper does not explicitly say ferrocene was present in the analyte solution as an internal standard. This tolerance would rescue the Polymers 2013, J. Phys. Chem. B 2022, and Electrochimica Acta 2016 rows; without it, those rows remain parked. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn30view2Ņłéturn49view2Ņłéturn29view0Ņłü

I deliberately **did not** apply other possible leniencies. I did not promote rows reported explicitly vs **Ag/AgCl**, even if the paper also discussed ferrocene calibration, which is why DTPŌĆæPhŌĆæPyr remained excluded. I also did not promote rows where the accessible text did not let me safely resolve a **canonical SMILES** or even the exact side-chain identity from a structure figure, which affects M1ŌĆōM6 and the DTP series in the parked CSV. Finally, I did not promote rows where the value was more clearly a **polymerization threshold or growth setpoint** than an auditable first molecular oxidation label. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn33view2Ņłéturn27view3Ņłéturn49view0Ņłü

Open limitations remain. The visible-accessible text was enough to establish a useful provisional pool, but not enough to convert several promising leads into strict benchmark-ready rows.
