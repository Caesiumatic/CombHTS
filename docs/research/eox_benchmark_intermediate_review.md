# Monomer Oxidation Benchmark Review for xTB Calibration

## Executive summary

I reviewed the literature that was recoverable through primary articles, thesis PDFs, and article mirrors with enough electrochemical detail to audit monomer oxidation labels rather than polymer-film or deposition values. The usable source set was materially smaller than the target space because many electropolymerization papers report only deposition windows, film redox features, or values vs Ag/Ag⁺/Ag wire/pseudo-reference without an internal calibration that would justify conversion to Ag/AgCl. In particular, the accessible EDOS paper reports EDOS monomer anodic peaks but the accessible text does not recover a traceable reference electrode; the furan and 2-methylfuran paper reports clean monomer peaks but only vs Ag⁰/Ag⁺; and the thiophene-oligomer study reports explicit oxidation potentials vs Ag|AgCl but those are best interpreted as onset-like values from the authors’ polarization/electropolymerization framework rather than first-scan peak maxima. citeturn13view0turn18view0turn25view0turn42view3

From the material I could audit conservatively, I assembled **26 proposed raw rows**, of which **19 are calibration-eligible** under a conservative but usable ontology, and **18 collapsed monomer/solvent groups**. The benchmark therefore **does not meet** the requested `>=30` clean collapsed-group target. The main bottlenecks were: missing traceable reference electrodes for otherwise attractive monomer datasets; inaccessible or poorly documented primary sources for plain EDOT, plain selenophene, plain carbazole, pyrrole, and 3-hexylthiophene; and several cases where only electropolymerization setpoints or polymer-film redox signals were available rather than soluble-monomer oxidation features. citeturn13view0turn18view0turn40search2turn42view3

A key scientific finding is that **label hygiene matters more than row count**. For example, thiophene in acetonitrile appears at **2.05 V vs Ag/AgCl** as a first anodic oxidation value in a direct thiophene-derivative comparison table, but at **1.50 V vs Ag|AgCl** in the oligothiophene study’s “experimental oxidation potentials.” Those values are not interchangeable. The former behaves like an **oxidation peak**; the latter behaves like an **oxidation onset / electropolymerization-threshold-style value**. Averaging them would contaminate the calibration target. citeturn42view3turn25view0turn26view1

## Existing-row audit

Because the current repo file itself was not attached in this chat, I audited the seed-row *classes* you described rather than individual file UUIDs. The practical implication is that the recommendations below are suitable for a patch, but Codex should still diff them against the current `data/benchmark.csv` before writing. The most important result is that several seed rows should be **replaced by better-located direct-Ag/AgCl values** or **kept only as excluded provenance**.

For **thiophene**, I recommend **modifying** the existing record set. If the current main thiophene row is a nonaqueous-SCE-converted value, I would demote it and instead anchor the benchmark with the direct Ag/AgCl comparator row from Cihaner and Önal, where Table 1 explicitly reports the “first anodic oxidation value” of thiophene in `0.1 M TBABF4/acetonitrile` as **2.05 V vs Ag/AgCl**, with the caption also giving `100 mV/s` and Ag/AgCl as the reference. I would keep a second thiophene row from Camarada *only* as `monomer_oxidation_onset`, because its 1.50 V value is a different label type and should not be numerically merged with the peak row. citeturn42view3turn42view2turn25view0turn26view1

For **selenophene / EDOS-class rows**, I recommend **excluding** accessible seed rows unless the repo already has a primary source with an exact locator and explicit reference electrode. The accessible EDOS paper clearly states that EDOS monomer shows irreversible anodic peaks at **1.22 and 1.75 V** in acetonitrile at **50 mV s⁻¹**, and compares EDOT as **1.4 V under the same conditions**, but the accessible text does **not** recover the reference electrode from the JACS communication excerpt; the key peak values are pushed to Supporting Information Figure S7, which was not recoverable here with explicit reference metadata. That is not strong enough for calibration. citeturn13view0turn14view3

For **EDOT**, I recommend **downgrading to provisional / excluded** unless your current row is already backed by a primary source with explicit reference, locator, and clear monomer label. The accessible secondary/open snippets support that EDOT is oxidized in roughly the expected low-potential regime, but not with the full provenance standard needed here. In the accessible EDOS comparison text, EDOT is “1.4 V under the same conditions,” but the reference is still not recovered; in a PEDOT growth study on silicon nanowires, the first-scan EDOT-to-PEDOT oxidation appears near **1.1 V**, but that is embedded in a polymer-growth context rather than a clean benchmark monomer row. citeturn13view0turn41search18

For **carbazole**, I recommend **excluding or replacing** any seed row that depends on an unverified nonaqueous `SCE -> Ag/AgCl` conversion. I did recover direct-Ag/AgCl carbazole-derivative monomer rows from a METU thesis, but not a plain-carbazole primary row that satisfies all provenance requirements in the accessible material. Those derivative rows are useful additions, but they do not rescue an uncertain plain-carbazole seed row. citeturn31view0turn32view2

For **3-hexylthiophene**, I agree with the repo’s current instinct: **keep excluded** unless the primary source, reference electrode, electrolyte, and scan rate can be revalidated. Given your note about internal-reference/electrolyte inconsistency and an implausible scan rate, that row should remain a provenance row rather than calibration data.

## Reference conversion audit and bibliography

### Conversion audit

The only conversions I actually used in the proposed calibration rows were **zero-offset identity mappings**, because that is the only conservative path that remained auditable here. Where a source directly reported potentials “vs Ag/AgCl” or “vs Ag|AgCl, KCl(sat’d),” I set

\[
E_{\text{vs Ag/AgCl}} = E_{\text{native}} + 0.00
\]

which simply means **the converted value is the same as the reported value** because the source already used an Ag/AgCl reference. citeturn42view3turn26view1

| original reference | target reference | solvent / medium | offset used | source | uncertainty / caveat | rows affected |
|---|---|---|---:|---|---|---|
| Ag/AgCl (3 M NaCl aq) | Ag/AgCl | acetonitrile | 0.00 V | Cihaner/Önal | direct reported scale; still a nonaqueous junction situation | thiophene, DiMeOTh, DiBrTh, BrMeOTh |
| Ag\|AgCl, KCl(sat’d) | Ag/AgCl | acetonitrile, dichloromethane | 0.00 V | Camarada et al. | direct reported scale; saturation specification available | thiophene oligomer series rows |
| Ag/AgCl unspecified | Ag/AgCl | ACN, DCM, mixed media | 0.00 V | METU thesis rows | exact chloride concentration / fill solution not recovered from snippet | carbazole- and fluorene-derivative rows |
| Ag⁰/Ag⁺ | Ag/AgCl | acetonitrile | not used | Demirboga/Önal | no traceable internal conversion recovered | furan, 2-methylfuran excluded |
| unrecovered reference | Ag/AgCl | acetonitrile | not used | Patra et al. | reference metadata not recovered from accessible excerpt | EDOS, EDOT comparison rows excluded |

I did **not** use nonaqueous `SCE -> Ag/AgCl` conversions in any eligible row. The relevant caution paper is Pavlishchuk and Addison, who explicitly emphasize that reference-electrode comparisons in acetonitrile are often inaccurate if handled casually. For aqueous comparisons, the familiar room-temperature difference between SCE (+0.241 V vs SHE) and saturated Ag/AgCl (+0.197 V vs SHE) is about **+0.044 V**, but I recommend using that only for clearly aqueous cases, not for the mixed nonaqueous seed rows you flagged. citeturn40search2turn40search1turn40search13

1. stratify by label type, or  
2. prefer peak rows when both peak and onset exist for the same monomer/solvent.

- `native_potential_V + conversion_to_AgAgCl_V = exp_Eox_V_vs_AgAgCl` within 5 mV;  
- `calibration_eligible = true` implies `label_type in {monomer_oxidation_peak, monomer_oxidation_onset}`;

## Red flags and unresolved questions

The biggest red flag is the **thiophene onset/peak gap** in acetonitrile: **1.50 V** in the oligothiophene paper versus **2.05 V** in the direct comparator table. That discrepancy is too large to explain away as ordinary experimental scatter; it is a label-definition problem. I would not let the calibration pipeline collapse those values into one training target. If your current code aggregates by `(monomer, solvent)` without respecting `label_type`, that behavior should be changed before retraining. citeturn42view3turn25view0

A second red flag is that several otherwise attractive families remain blocked by **reference provenance**, not by chemistry. The EDOS/EDOT accessible source material is chemically informative but not benchmark-safe because the reference electrode is not recoverable from the accessible excerpt. Likewise, the furan paper is experimentally clear but unusable for Ag/AgCl calibration without a defensible Ag⁰/Ag⁺ conversion in acetonitrile. These are precisely the kinds of rows that should remain visible in the CSV as provenance so future contributors do not unknowingly “recover” them by guesswork. citeturn13view0turn18view0turn40search2

A third red flag is that **thesis-derived calibration rows** are useful but materially weaker than journal-primary rows. I kept several as Tier B because the electrochemical labels and conditions are explicit, but they are appropriate only if you accept primary theses as admissible benchmark sources. If your group prefers peer-reviewed primaries only, then the eligible set shrinks further and the benchmark becomes even smaller. In that case, I would recommend accepting the smaller clean set rather than padding with ambiguous literature. citeturn31view0turn35view0

Finally, I think your repo should seriously consider a future migration to an **Fc/Fc⁺ master scale** for nonaqueous electrochemistry. The current Ag/AgCl target is still workable when a source directly reports Ag/AgCl, but the literature is much richer in Ag/Ag⁺, Ag wire, and Fc/Fc⁺ conventions than in clean nonaqueous Ag/AgCl benchmarking. Pavlishchuk/Addison is useful for caution and limited conversions, but a ferrocene-centered master scale would align better with how synthetic electrochemistry papers are actually reported. That migration would require a separate design decision and should be signed off by the project lead before implementation. citeturn40search2turn24search6
