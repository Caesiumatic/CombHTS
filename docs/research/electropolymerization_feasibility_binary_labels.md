# Binary Electropolymerization-Feasibility Labels: A Vetted Ground-Truth Set for Computational Screen Validation

## TL;DR
- This vetting yielded **18 CLEAN YES** and **9 CLEAN NO** binary labels (27 total in the clean set), each tied to a primary CV/deposition report with a DOI; a further ~6 rows were quarantined as AMBIGUOUS or off-baseline and listed in the appendix.
- The dominant primary sources are the Diaz polypyrrole papers (J. Electroanal. Chem. 1981), the EDOS solvent/electrolyte study (RSC Adv. 2020, 10.1039/D0RA01436B), the oligofuran study (Chem. Sci. 2015, 10.1039/C4SC02664K), the substituted-carbazole coupling study (J. Electroanal. Chem. 2021, 10.1016/j.jelechem.2021.115356), and the OMIEC electropolymerization review (Chem. Rev. 2026, 10.1021/acs.chemrev.5c00183) for consensus framing.
- **The NO count (9) is too small and too medium-heterogeneous to credibly support a >85% accuracy claim.** The validation metric should remain "not-yet-computable" until ≥20–25 defensible negatives in the standard MeCN/TBAPF6-on-Pt baseline are gathered; the current set is dominated by easy positives and would let a trivial "always-YES" classifier score ~67%.

## Key Findings
- Electropolymerizability splits cleanly along radical-cation chemistry: monomers with low oxidation potential and free α/coupling positions (EDOT, EDOS, EDOP, pyrrole, 3-alkylthiophenes, terthiophene, bithiophene, carbazole) form films readily; monomers whose radical cation is either too stable (3-tert-butylcarbazole, triphenylamine) or whose coupling product is a discrete dimer (TPA → tetraphenylbenzidine) do NOT form films. The TPA case is supported verbatim by Hsiao, J. Polym. Sci. A 2016 (10.1002/pola.28124): "the one-electron oxidation of unsubstituted TPA causes the generation of reactive radical cations, which typically undergo irreversible dimerization to tetraphenylbenzidine (TPB) … exclusively in the para position to the nitrogen centers."
- **Medium is decisive and is itself a feature the screen must encode.** The same monomer flips outcome with solvent/electrolyte: terthiophene gives a film in MeCN but only a decaying single oxidation peak in DMF; aniline films in aqueous H2SO4 but fails on Pt at low concentration in neutral pH and gives no film on mild steel in MeCN/LiClO4; furan gives only over-oxidized low-quality material in standard MeCN but a free-standing film in BFEE.
- A single anodic CV peak is NOT evidence of film formation. The carbazole study quantifies the boundary cleanly: a radical-cation dimerization rate kdim ≈ 1.5×10⁷ M⁻¹·s⁻¹ (3-ethylcarbazole) gives "a thick adherent conducting polymer film," whereas kdim ≤ 1.5×10⁶ M⁻¹·s⁻¹ (3-tert-butylcarbazole, 3,6-di-tert-butylcarbazole) gives "no efficient electropolymerization" despite a clear oxidation peak. TPA, ProDOP-br-C3, and thiophene-3-acetic acid show the same "oxidizes but no film" failure mode the screen must not mis-score.
- Water and nucleophiles are the dominant film-killers for thiophenes (radical-cation quenching/over-oxidation), whereas for pyrrole a trace of water *helps* — an asymmetry the screen should not treat uniformly.

### Per-class commentary

**Thiophenes.** The unsubstituted parent is the hard case: its monomer oxidation potential (~1.6–2.0 V vs SCE in MeCN) sits near the solvent/electrolyte limit, so over-oxidation and nucleophilic quenching (especially by water) compete with chain growth — the classic "polythiophene paradox." Quality films of *polythiophene itself* therefore require oligomeric precursors (bi-/terthiophene), Lewis-acid BFEE media, or strictly anhydrous conditions. By contrast EDOT, ProDOT, 3-methylthiophene, 3-hexylthiophene, 3-methoxythiophene, 3,4-dimethoxythiophene, bithiophene and terthiophene all electropolymerize readily in dry MeCN/TBA-salt because their lower oxidation potentials and electron-rich rings stabilize coupling. Negatives in this class are medium-driven (thiophene in water; terthiophene in DMF) or substituent-driven (nucleophilic –COOH/–OH side groups quench the cation).

**Pyrroles.** The most robust film-formers in the set. Pyrrole, N-methylpyrrole, N-phenylpyrrole all give adherent films (Diaz), and EDOP polymerizes at exceptionally low potential. N-substitution does *not* block film growth here because coupling occurs at the 2,5-(α) ring positions, not nitrogen — distinguishing pyrroles from arylamines. The genuine pyrrole-class negatives are steric/solubility failures (short branched ProDOP side chains giving soluble products) and α,α′-disubstituted pyrroles that block coupling. Note the inverted "water effect": a trace of water *accelerates* pyrrole film growth in MeCN, opposite to thiophene.

**Furans.** The textbook difficult case. Furan's high oxidation potential drives ring-opening/over-oxidation, so the standard MeCN/TBA medium gives only low-quality, short-conjugation material — a defensible NO. Films require either oligofuran precursors (terfuran, <1.0 V vs SCE) or BFEE. In *pure* BFEE, however, the strong Lewis acidity drives non-conjugated additive polymerization (benzofuran → white, non-conducting), another NO unless moderated with PEG. Furan is the cleanest example of "monomer oxidizes but does not give a film in the baseline medium."

**Selenophenes.** Behave like electron-rich thiophenes. EDOS electropolymerizes well in MeCN (best with TBAClO4); selenophene itself forms films/copolymers at constant potential. No defensible selenophene *negative* was located in standard media — a coverage gap.

**Anilines.** Strongly medium-dependent. Aniline, o-toluidine, o-anisidine and diphenylamine all form films in *acidic aqueous* media (the protonation/radical-cation balance is essential). The negatives are highly informative: aniline does not polymerize on Pt at low concentration in neutral pH, and gives no film on mild steel in aprotic MeCN/LiClO4. Triphenylamine is a true *chemical* negative — its radical cation couples only to the discrete dimer tetraphenylbenzidine and does not propagate to a film unless the para positions are engineered. This TPA/diphenylamine contrast (dimer-only vs film) is the single most valuable arylamine distinction for the screen.

**Carbazoles.** Couple at the 3,6-positions through a radical-cation dimerization that then grows. Carbazole, 3-ethylcarbazole, N-vinylcarbazole and most 3-substituted carbazoles give films. The defensible negatives are electronic/steric: 3-tert-butylcarbazole and 3,6-di-tert-butylcarbazole do not electropolymerize because their radical cations are too stable to couple efficiently — the same paper provides the quantitative dividing line (kdim ≈ 1.5×10⁷ → film; kdim ≤ 1.5×10⁶ → no film) and matched YES/NO rows under identical conditions, making it the highest-value single reference in this set.

**Fluorenes.** Most polyfluorenes in the literature are made by Suzuki coupling, not electropolymerization; direct anodic electropolymerization of 9,9-dialkylfluorene monomers is poorly documented and prone to cross-linking/defects. The defensible fluorene-class YES here is the arylamine-functionalized fluorene FDA (in DCM). 9,9-dioctylfluorene monomer direct electropolymerization is excluded as AMBIGUOUS (see appendix).

## Recommendations
1. **Mark the >85% accuracy validation metric "not-yet-computable" for now.** With 18 YES and 9 NO, the class balance (~67% positive) means a degenerate "always-YES" baseline already scores ~67%; an 85% threshold is only ~3 mislabeled negatives away from being gamed. The headline number would be dominated by easy positives.
2. **Target ≥20–25 defensible negatives, concentrated in the baseline MeCN/TBAPF6-on-Pt regime,** so that YES and NO are compared on the same medium the screen assumes. Priority hunting grounds: α,α′-disubstituted thiophenes/pyrroles (coupling-site blocked), additional stable-radical-cation arylamines and carbazoles (e.g., 3,6-di-tert-butylcarbazole, para-blocked triarylamines), end-capped oligomers, and nucleophile-bearing monomers (–OH, –NH2, –COOH) that oxidize but do not deposit.
3. **Stratify the metric.** Report accuracy separately for (a) chemical negatives (monomer cannot form a film in any standard medium: furan, 3-tert-butylcarbazole, TPA, ProDOP-br-C3) and (b) medium-specific negatives (wrong solvent/electrolyte/electrode: aniline-neutral-Pt, aniline-MeCN-steel, thiophene-water, terthiophene-DMF, benzofuran-pure-BFEE). A screen that encodes the triad should be judged mainly on (b); a screen that ignores medium should be flagged, because more than half the negatives here are medium-driven.
4. **Quarantine flagged rows from the headline metric:** BFEE/Lewis-acid media (rows 21, 29) and pseudo-reference-electrode rows (6, 7, 31) complicate comparison to the standard MeCN/TBAPF6-on-Pt baseline. Report a sensitivity analysis with and without them.
5. **Benchmarks that would change the recommendation:** once the clean NO set reaches ≥20 with ≥10 in the exact baseline medium, and **balanced accuracy** (mean of per-class accuracy) — not raw accuracy — exceeds 85% with the trivial baseline below ~55%, the claim becomes defensible. Until then, report balanced accuracy and the full confusion matrix, never a single accuracy figure.

## Caveats / Excluded & Ambiguous Appendix
The following were dropped from the clean label set:

- **9,9-dioctylfluorene monomer, direct electropolymerization (MeCN/TBAPF6/Pt) — AMBIGUOUS/EXCLUDED.** Literature overwhelmingly makes polyfluorenes by chemical (Suzuki) coupling; direct anodic film growth from the dialkylfluorene monomer is not cleanly documented and is prone to cross-linking. No defensible binary call.
- **3-methoxythiophene / 3,4-dimethoxythiophene in aqueous SDS micellar medium — NO (soluble oligomers) but EXCLUDED from clean baseline** because the outcome is medium-specific and the same monomers are YES in MeCN (row 11); retained only as a within-paper contrast (10.1016/S0379-6779(01)00344-7).
- **N-octyl / long-chain N-substituted pyrroles — AMBIGUOUS.** Long alkyl chains slow deposition drastically and some give only "colorless or slightly blackish thin film"; the YES/NO boundary is not crisp.
- **PEDOT/polythiophene over-oxidation above ~1.4 V — NOT a monomer negative.** This is film *degradation* after successful growth, not a failure to polymerize; noted in commentary, not tabled.
- **Thiophene-3-acetic acid / 2-(3-thienyl)ethanol — supportive but off-scope.** These nucleophile-bearing thiophenes oxidize but give soluble products/no film, illustrating the mechanism; excluded because they are not in the screening-library scope.

**Source-quality and honesty notes:** (i) Several reference electrodes are recorded as "not located" rather than guessed — do not infer them. (ii) DOIs marked "(verify)" (rows 27, 28, 29) were reconstructed from confirmed Elsevier PIIs and should be resolver-checked before publication. (iii) The furan-monomer negative (row 23) rests on the oligofuran paper's framing of prior literature (its refs 5/11/12), not on a new furan experiment in that paper — it is a consensus statement, hence Tier A on the chemistry but flagged as review-framed. (iv) Aniline (row 17) and o-anisidine (row 19) are given Tier B because they rest on consensus/secondary statements; dedicated primary CV-growth papers should replace these before final use. (v) Row 25 (TPA): no single primary paper states verbatim "TPA forms no film"; the defensible, verbatim-supported claim is that the TPA radical cation couples only to the tetraphenylbenzidine dimer (10.1002/pola.28124) and that para-blocked triarylamines do not couple at all (10.1021/jp208076z) — film growth requires multi-TPA monomers with free para positions.
