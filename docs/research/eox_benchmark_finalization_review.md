## Reference conversion audit and source evidence table

No **nonzero** reference conversion survived into the strict benchmark-ready set. That is deliberate. The first-pass report already identified multiple rows where speculative conversion would do more harm than good, especially for `Ag0/Ag+` in acetonitrile and for unrecovered nonaqueous references. I therefore retained only direct Ag/AgCl-type reports in the benchmark-ready CSV. The reference-conversion rule applied here was the one stated above,

\[
E_{\mathrm{vs\ Ag/AgCl}} = E_{\mathrm{native}} + \Delta E_{\mathrm{ref}},
\]

where \(\Delta E_{\mathrm{ref}}\) is simply the reference-offset term. In every retained benchmark-ready row, \(\Delta E_{\mathrm{ref}} = 0.00\ \mathrm{V}\) because the source already reported against an Ag/AgCl-type reference. I used standard aqueous reference-electrode values only for cautionary context, not to force nonaqueous conversions into the dataset. At 25 °C, the saturated calomel electrode is about `+0.244 V vs SHE`, while a saturated Ag/AgCl electrode is about `+0.197 V vs SHE`; those numbers explain why a simple aqueous `SCE -> Ag/AgCl` offset is roughly `+0.044 to +0.047 V`, but that familiar shortcut is not reliable enough to rescue nonaqueous rows without source-internal calibration. citeturn30search0turn30search1turn30search2
